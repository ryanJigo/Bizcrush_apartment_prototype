Things to Consider in Seattle

Important transit, commute, and housing information for the Seattle metro area.

East Link Light Rail (Line 2)

Sound Transit's East Link extension connects Bellevue and Redmond directly to Seattle via light rail, making cross-lake commutes faster and more convenient.

Key Benefits

  • Direct connection from Redmond to downtown Seattle without traffic delays
  • Multiple stations in Bellevue's urban core and Spring District
  • Integrated with existing Link Light Rail network for seamless transfers
  • Runs approximately every 8-10 minutes during peak hours
  • Travel time: Redmond to downtown Seattle in about 45 minutes

Major Stations

  • Redmond Technology Center — Near Microsoft campus
  • Downtown Redmond — Central Redmond
  • Overlake Village — Shopping and dining area
  • Bellevue Downtown — Bellevue city center
  • South Bellevue — I-90 corridor access
  • Mercer Island — Island station
  • Connects to Seattle stations including Capitol Hill, University of Washington, and Westlake

East Link Light Rail network connecting Redmond, Bellevue, and Seattle

Commute Impact: If you work in Bellevue or Redmond (especially near tech campuses), consider proximity to light rail stations when choosing an apartment. The Capella property in Redmond is relatively close to the East Link corridor.

Income-Restricted Properties

Some Seattle-area apartments offer below-market rents through income-restricted programs. These units are reserved for low to moderate-income households and can provide significant savings, but require income verification.

How It Works

  • Properties set aside a percentage of units for qualified households
  • Rent is capped at a percentage of Area Median Income (AMI)
  • Applicants must provide proof of income (pay stubs, tax returns, etc.)
  • Income limits vary by unit size and household size
  • Once approved, you typically recertify annually
